ANNUAL REPORT — GREENLEAF RENEWABLE ENERGY COOPERATIVE
Fiscal Year 2023

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------
Greenleaf Renewable Energy Cooperative (GREC) concluded fiscal year 2023 with its strongest
financial performance to date.  Total revenue reached €42.7 million, representing a 17 %
year-on-year increase driven by higher average electricity prices and an expanded installed
capacity base.

OPERATIONAL HIGHLIGHTS
-----------------------
Installed capacity (end of year) : 318 MW
  - Wind farms              : 194 MW  (12 sites across northern Europe)
  - Solar photovoltaic      : 101 MW  (7 utility-scale parks)
  - Run-of-river hydro      :  23 MW  (3 stations in the Alps)

Total energy generated in 2023   : 741 GWh
  - Wind                    : 498 GWh
  - Solar                   : 187 GWh
  - Hydro                   :  56 GWh

Average capacity factor           : 26.6 %
  - Wind                    : 29.3 %
  - Solar                   : 21.1 %
  - Hydro                   : 27.8 %

CO₂ emissions avoided             : 327,240 tonnes
  (based on a grid emission factor of 441 g CO₂/kWh for the relevant mix)

STRATEGY AND OUTLOOK
---------------------
GREC aims to reach 500 MW of installed capacity by end of 2026 through a pipeline of
approved projects totalling 182 MW:
  - Nordsee V offshore wind extension  (68 MW, expected commissioning Q3 2025)
  - Iberia Solar Portfolio III         (74 MW, expected commissioning Q1 2026)
  - Rhône Hydro upgrade                (40 MW, expected commissioning Q4 2025)

The cooperative will also pilot a 10 MWh battery storage facility alongside the
Iberia Solar Portfolio III project, exploring hybrid dispatchable generation.
